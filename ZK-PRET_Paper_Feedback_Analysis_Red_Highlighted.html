REVIEWER 1 FEEDBACK (Highlighted in Red)

MAJOR REVISIONS REQUIRED

1. Methodological Transparency

  • Greater methodological transparency around circuit construction and security
  • Brief description of how conditional branches (e.g., 2×3×2×3 paths) are compiled into arithmetic circuits and optimized

2. Evaluation and Evidence

  • At least preliminary results on real or semi-real datasets
  • Small-scale proof-of-concept on anonymized or semi-synthetic real-world process logs (e.g., public dataset of clinical workflows)
  • Clarify whether synthetic scenarios include benchmarks drawn from real clinical-trial data flows or industry standards

3. Security Analysis

  • Security analysis: Beyond proof-size and performance, an adversarial analysis—how robust are these circuits to malicious proof-forgery or side-channel timing attacks?
  • Discussion of threat models and mitigations

4. Literature Review

  • Cite and briefly compare with contemporaneous efforts in cryptographic healthcare auditing (for instance, any existing "proofs of compliance" prototypes in clinical trials)

5. Structure and Clarity Improvements

  • Enhanced clarity in figures and narrative flow
  • Consider moving the "Agentic AI Challenge" section earlier—perhaps as part of the Introduction
  • Add "Key Contributions" bullet list at the end of the Introduction
  • Figures 1–4 and Tables 1–5 contain wealth of information, but some diagrams (e.g., circuit execution diagrams) lack sufficient captions explaining axes and symbols

REVIEWER 2 FEEDBACK (Highlighted in Red)

FORMATTING AND TECHNICAL ISSUES

1. Citation Format

  • Needs Vancouver format for citations/references (looks like footnotes rather than parentheses)

2. Experimental Details

  • Better explanation of how this would be further tested in an experimental environment and what comparators may be
  • Comparing/contrasting with prior state-of-the-art technologies with similar functions
  • Most of the evidence seems to be largely conceptual at this point

3. Commercial Tools Clarification

  • Are Object Management Group and ZK-PRET Business Process Prover commercial tools/businesses? Not clear how they were developed

4. Grammar and Language Issues

  • Subject/verb agreement issue in this sentence: "AI systems—with their autonomous decision-making, probabilistic reasoning, and emergent behaviors—has exponentially amplified these compliance challenges"

5. Figure Completeness

  • Is Figure 3 complete? I see a placeholder for it but the diagram wasn't in the file reviewed
  • Similar for Fig. 4

6. AI/LLM Usage Declaration

  • Found the "Timeline Compression Analysis" section confusing and have concerns that authors may have over-relied on LLM use in this section
  • Declare clearly any use of AI/LLMs in writing or editing this piece

7. Formatting Issues

  • Several sections could be re-formatted as lists or tables for clarity
  • Strange formatting with check marks: "In multi-state telemedicine, static rules might verify that a rural hospital can prescribe medications (✓), cross-state practice requires valid license (✓)..."
  • Formatting issues in framework deployment section: "The framework's modular architecture supports deployment across diverse healthcare environments: - Small Clinical Practices..."

8. Missing Citations

  • Table 5 is missing some citations for its claims

9. Policy and Business Implications

  • Is there thought around how these proofs would be accepted by CMS/OCR/FDA auditors? Would be an interesting implication for policy and business
